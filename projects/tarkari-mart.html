Tarkari Mart: Farm to Taste

Optimizing Local Produce Supply Chain

Category: Supply Chain Analytics Tools: SQL, Tableau, Excel Date: October 2023

1. Project Background & Business Problem

Tarkari Mart, a local produce delivery service, faced challenges with inefficient supply chain operations. This led to delays in delivery, increased waste of perishable goods, and fluctuating customer satisfaction. The primary business problem was to identify bottlenecks in the supply chain and propose data-driven solutions to enhance efficiency and reduce operational costs.

2. Approach & Tools Used

My approach involved a multi-faceted analysis:

  • Data Collection & Cleansing: Gathered historical data from delivery logs, inventory systems, and customer feedback databases. Used SQL queries to extract relevant datasets and perform initial cleansing to ensure data quality.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Employed SQL for aggregate queries and Excel for initial visualizations to understand delivery routes, peak order times, inventory turnover, and customer complaint patterns.
  • Supply Chain Mapping & Bottleneck Identification: Utilized Tableau to create interactive dashboards visualizing the entire supply chain, from farm sourcing to final delivery. This helped pinpoint areas of significant delay (e.g., inefficient sorting, suboptimal routing, high return rates).
  • Performance Metric Development: Defined key performance indicators (KPIs) such as average delivery time, waste percentage, order fulfillment rate, and customer satisfaction score.
  • Predictive Analysis (Basic): Used Excel's forecasting tools to predict demand fluctuations based on historical sales data, aiding in better inventory management.

Tools Used: SQL (PostgreSQL), Tableau Desktop, Microsoft Excel.

4. Insights Generated & Business Impact

Through this analysis, several key insights were uncovered:

  • Identified that 30% of delivery delays were due to inefficient packing processes at the distribution hub, rather than external traffic issues.
  • Revealed a significant correlation between a specific supplier and higher rates of perishable waste, leading to a review of sourcing strategies.
  • Highlighted a 15% increase in customer complaints on weekends, largely attributed to understaffing in delivery personnel.

The implementation of recommendations based on these insights resulted in:

  • 10% reduction in average delivery time within two months.
  • 18% decrease in perishable waste over a quarter.
  • Improved customer satisfaction scores by 7%.
